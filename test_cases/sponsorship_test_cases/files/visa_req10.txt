What You’ll Do


As an Entry-Level Software Engineer, you’ll join a multi-disciplinary team where you’ll:

Design, develop, and test software for advanced defense and space systems.
Contribute to full lifecycle engineering—from coding and debugging to integration and system testing.
Collaborate with system, hardware, and test engineers in a fast-paced, mission-driven environment.
Support field demonstrations, software installations, and system-level testing.
Gain exposure to advanced technologies and tools while building the foundation for a long-term engineering career.



Your First Year: The Year One (YO) Experience


Every new engineer joins our Year One (YO) program—a curated experience designed to help you thrive from day one.

Connect with peers and leaders across the company through team-building events and networking sessions.
Participate in workshops, training modules, and mentorship opportunities that accelerate your technical and professional growth.
Gain a dedicated peer mentor and support team to guide your first year as a BAE Systems engineer.
Participate in rotations to build your technical skillset.



Why You’ll Love Working Here


Every other Friday off with our 9/80 schedule.
Meaningful mission: Your work directly supports the safety and success of those who serve.
Mentorship and career growth: Structured support from experienced engineers and leaders.
Collaborative culture: We celebrate diverse ideas, encourage curiosity, and value innovation.
Excellent benefits package that includes tuition reimbursement for advanced degrees.



Required Education, Experience, & Skills


BS degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering or related field received
Less than one year of full-time professional software engineering experience (excluding internships)
Proficiency in one or more high level programming languages (e.g., C, C++, Java, Python)
Working knowledge of software design and code utilizing Object Oriented Analysis & Design (OOAD) techniques
needs security clearance
